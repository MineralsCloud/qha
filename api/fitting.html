1.6. Fitting Birch–Murnaghan equation of state¶

This module is one of the most important part of this package since it implements a robust Birch–Murnaghan EoS fitting for grid_interpolation modules’ use.

qha.fitting.polynomial_least_square_fitting(x, y, new_x, order=3)[source]¶

Free Energy is expanded to the order\(^\mathrm{th}\) order in strain.

Check the Wolfram Mathworld to find the method of calculating the coefficients.

Parameters:
  • x – Eulerian strain of calculated volumes (sparse).
  • y – Free energy of these calculated volumes (sparse).
  • new_x – Eulerian strain at a greater dense vector.
  • order (Optional[int]) – The order chose to fit Birch–Murnaghan EoS, default is 3, i.e., third-order Birch–Murnaghan EoS fitting.
Returns:

Free energy at a denser strain vector (denser volumes vector).
qha.fitting.birch_murnaghan_finite_strain_fitting(eulerian_strain, free_energy, strain, order=3)[source]¶

Calculate the F(T, V) for given strain. Free Energy is expanded to the order\(^\mathrm{th}\) order in strain.

Parameters:
  • eulerian_strain – Eulerian strain of calculated volumes (sparse).
  • free_energy – Free energy of these calculated volumes (sparse).
  • strain – Eulerian strain at a greater dense vector.
  • order (Optional[int]) – The order chose to fit Birch–Murnaghan EoS, default is 3, i.e., third-order Birch–Murnaghan EoS fitting.
Returns:

Free energy at a dense \((T, V)\) grid.
